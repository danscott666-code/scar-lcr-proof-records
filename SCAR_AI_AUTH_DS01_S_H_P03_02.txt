Walking is a form of exercise that is accessible to most unlike many other forms of exercise. No particular skill set, equipment or extra strength is required above that of being able to stand up and move your feet one after the other. Due to the nature of walking it is slow and therefore low impact on joints and low chance of injury with a higher chance of more people being able to participate in it. Despite it being low impact and slow it still has relatively high health benefits including reducing heart disease, stress, other illness and diseases and increasing muscle strength and fitness and producing endorphins to improve mood and mental health. It is a good start to help with weight management and can also be a gateway to moving into more challenging exercise for example jogging then running as stamina and confidence builds and potentially if required weight is lost or stabilises. That all being said I personally believe these benefits would only truly be accessed if you can manage to do enough steps, get your heart rate up sufficiently and maintain doing it regularly for a prolonged period, as a minimum I believe ten thousand steps per day are recommended. Start off slow with the intention of increasing speed and distance in the longer term.
