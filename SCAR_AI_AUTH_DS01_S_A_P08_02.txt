Preparing for a job interview is a deliberate process that combines research, rehearsal, and practical organisation. Start by understanding the company at a structural level, its products, market position, recent developments, and culture, so your answers align with its priorities rather than sounding generic. Analyse the job description line by line and map your experience directly to the required competencies, forming clear, evidence-based examples that demonstrate impact, not just activity. Anticipate common questions and practise concise, structured responses, ideally using a method that shows context, action, and measurable results. Equally important is preparing your own questions, as this signals genuine engagement and allows you to assess whether the role fits your objectives. On the practical side, plan logistics in advance, including travel time or technical setup for remote interviews, and ensure your attire matches the organisation's norms. Mental preparation matters as much as content; aim for calm, controlled delivery rather than memorised scripts. Finally, review your CV thoroughly so nothing on it catches you off guard, and approach the conversation as a two-way evaluation rather than a test, which tends to produce a more confident and natural performance.
