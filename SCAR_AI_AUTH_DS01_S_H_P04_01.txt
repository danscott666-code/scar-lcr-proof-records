I have never had to work from home but I feel you could get distracted if you have a partner asking you to do things around the house, or kids disturbing you, being noisy. Also motivation could be hard with the opportunity to stay in bed, to watch a movie or be on your phone as you are not being monitored by a manager. Potentially staying up late, knowing that you do not have to travel, consequently feeling tired the next day which could affect concentration and productivity levels. There could be the other case where you do not stop working because there are no defined limits. You are at home, you can work without thinking you should stop, you make food and eat where you are working and then you forget to get out of the house. You may not see anyone for prolonged periods and therefore miss social interaction and it could affect your mental health. There are also some obvious positives such as flexibility, no commute time, more time with family and a more comfortable work environment with quick access to all your home comforts.
