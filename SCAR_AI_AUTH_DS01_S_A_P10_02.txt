Online learning has become a major part of modern education, offering clear advantages alongside notable limitations. One of its strongest benefits is flexibility, allowing learners to access material at their own pace and schedule, which is particularly useful for those balancing work, family, or other commitments. It also broadens access to education, removing geographical barriers and often reducing costs related to travel, accommodation, and physical resources. Additionally, digital platforms can provide a wide range of multimedia tools that enhance engagement and cater to different learning styles. However, this model is not without its drawbacks. A lack of direct, in-person interaction can lead to feelings of isolation and reduce opportunities for spontaneous discussion, which often deepens understanding. Online learning also demands a high level of self-discipline and time management, and not all students are equally equipped for this level of independence. Technical issues, such as unreliable internet access or unfamiliarity with digital tools, can further hinder progress. Moreover, certain subjects that rely on hands-on practice or laboratory work are difficult to replicate effectively in a virtual environment, potentially limiting the depth of learning in those areas.
