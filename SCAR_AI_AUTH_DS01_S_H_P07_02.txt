I think as someone who probably has ADHD, sleep is key in my coping strategy. I use a lot of energy, so sleep is critical. I stay up late even when I need sleep and I also have an early start. At night time, I find peace even though this can mean being distracted by YouTube etc. An issue for me, despite me causing my own problems by not sleeping, is I struggle to function if I don't get at least, four hours sleep. Usually, I would attempt to get one nap of at least thirty minutes. Ideally I'd prefer two to three hours. If I don't get enough sleep, I find that I'm moody. I'm grumpy, reactionary, easily frustrated and annoyed. It can also affect my driving and how I react to other road users. It affects how I parent and even how I communicate with friends. The affect is accumulative the more sleep I miss, the worse it becomes. Although not proven, sleeping actually helps to reduce my ADHD traits and is part of my coping mechanism, I also think food is intrinsically linked to this as I do forget or don't prioritise eating as I should and this has a compound affect.
