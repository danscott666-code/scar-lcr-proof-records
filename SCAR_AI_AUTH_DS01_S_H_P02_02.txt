A technique I am aware of but have not personally used is designing a schedule or planner where every lesson or subject is assigned a specific amount of time and every day is divided into study sessions, that is blocks, with specific rest or break periods included too. Each session time slot will have attributed to it specific topics that will be targeted for a dedicated amount of time. This way the whole syllabus can be covered in detail, hopefully leaving no gaps. There will also be specifically allotted time for past papers, self testing and even possibly coordinated study time with peers to gain insight from friends or a fresh perspective and new ideas and check if anything has been missed. I feel that using this method and planning in advance can greatly help to specifically reduce tension, anxiety and pressure by adding an element of calm and controlled organisation. Especially studying with peers can be very helpful as they may notice gaps or mistakes that you have missed or you yourself were not aware of when you are self checking. You cannot learn or prepare for something if you genuinely do not know you have missed it. This seems to me it could be the best route to success overall.
