Making a cup of tea starts with heating fresh water, as the quality of the water directly affects the taste, so use cold water from the tap rather than reboiling old water. Fill a kettle with this fresh water and bring it to a full boil. While the water is heating, take a clean cup and, if possible, warm it slightly by rinsing it with a little hot water and then discarding that water, as this helps maintain the temperature of the tea once it is made. Place a teabag into the cup, or if using loose tea, add an appropriate amount, typically about one teaspoon per cup. Once the kettle has boiled, carefully pour the hot water over the tea, ensuring the teabag or leaves are fully submerged. Allow the tea to steep for a few minutes, usually between three and five, depending on how strong you want it, occasionally moving the teabag or stirring gently to help the flavour infuse. After steeping, remove the teabag or strain out the leaves, pressing lightly to extract the remaining liquid without forcing out bitterness. At this point, you can add milk, sugar, or any preferred sweetener according to taste, stirring gently until everything is evenly mixed. The tea should now be hot, balanced in flavour, and ready to drink.
