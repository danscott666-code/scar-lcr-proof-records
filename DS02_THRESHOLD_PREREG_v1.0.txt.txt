SECTION 3 — NULL MODEL LOCK AND SAMPLING SPECIFICATION
3.1 Definition
The null model is defined in SCAR_AI_AUTH_DS01_MEASUREMENT_SPEC_v1.1 and represents unguided or random structure.
________________


3.2 Sampling Method
The null distribution of Theta(C) is generated as follows:
   * Minimum sample size: 100,000 independent samples OR sufficient sampling to achieve convergence (whichever is greater)
   * Generation method: identical to measurement specification
   * Random number generator: Mersenne Twister (MT19937)
   * Random seed: 1337
   * Sampling independence: each sample independently generated
Note: DS01 used 1,000 null samples per text. DS02 increases this to 100,000 to improve percentile stability, particularly at the 99.9th percentile. This change is pre-declared and does not constitute post-hoc adjustment.
________________


3.3 Convergence Requirement
Convergence is defined as:
   * percentile stability within ±0.1% tolerance
   * measured across at least two consecutive non-overlapping batches of equal size
No further sampling is permitted once convergence is achieved.
________________


3.4 Immutability
The null model and sampling procedure are fixed and may not be altered after this document is frozen.
________________


SECTION 4 — PRIMARY THRESHOLD DEFINITION (NON-CIRCULAR)
4.1 Principle
The classification threshold is defined independently of DS01 and DS02 observed data.
________________


4.2 Threshold Definition
The threshold is defined as:
The empirical 99.9th percentile of the null distribution of Theta(C).
________________


4.3 Percentile Computation Method
   * Method: empirical distribution
   * No interpolation permitted
   * Value selected as the smallest Theta(C) such that ≥99.9% of null samples fall below it
________________


4.4 Threshold Freeze
Once computed, the threshold value is fixed, recorded, and hashed.
Re-computation under any condition is strictly prohibited.
________________


4.5 Properties
   * Derived solely from null-generated data
   * Independent of all observed human and AI outputs
   * Non-adaptive and fixed prior to DS02
________________


SECTION 5 — PRIMARY OUTCOME
The primary outcome of DS02 is group-level statistical
separation between human and AI theta distributions.
The null hypothesis is that human and AI samples are
drawn from the same theta distribution.
The test is a two-sided permutation test with
pre-declared significance threshold p < 0.05.
Individual authorship classification is not claimed.
________________


Definition — COHERENCE
COHERENT means: exceeds null baseline.
It does not mean: human-authored.
________________

SECTION 7 — EVALUATION METRICS (FORMALLY DEFINED)
Metrics are computed with respect to the positive class (COHERENT):

   * Recall

SECTION 9 — HUMAN EVALUATION PROTOCOL
   * Minimum raters: 3 independent evaluators
   * Independence: no communication or influence
   * Blind conditions: no access to Theta values
   * Agreement rule: majority vote
Tie handling (fixed):
   * If split (e.g. 1–1–1), classify as NON-COHERENT
Rationale: conservative tie-breaking reduces false positive rate.
Human evaluation is observational and does not influence threshold.
