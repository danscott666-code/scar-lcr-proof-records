Sleep is a critical foundational building block for the human body and brain along with food, water and air. It acts as an aid for optimal mental performance by restoring everyday functions and almost acting as a daily reset. Without sleep or with a lack of sleep, focus and concentration are short lived and difficult to maintain especially over long periods. Being tired often leads to errors in work, lack of ideas, lack of motivation and sloppy work such as cutting corners due to lack of drive. Sleep can help increase stamina, helps muscles to build, rebuild and repair, reduces stress and dissipates cortisol from the blood, and helps you to decompress psychologically. Prolonged reduced sleep can lead to potential health issues throughout the body not limited to but including the heart and brain, such as cardiovascular disease or depression. Not getting enough sleep will truly adversely affect your life. I did hear of a study a year or so ago that told of a decrease in heart disease of between thirty and forty percent in people who napped during the day. I cannot quote the full statistics or data but I do genuinely recall it.
