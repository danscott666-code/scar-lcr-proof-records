Although I have not been to a library in many years, I can see why and how it can help a local community. Not everyone has the same resources and at a time when everything is so immediate, it takes us a step back. It helps people from a young age to feel the tactile nature of a book in their hand, to encourage them to read and fall in love with literature. It is not just fiction but also a place to do research for projects that children will have in school. It is parent dependent, but parents may go to a coffee morning in the local library where new parents come together with young kids. This then progresses over years, they take their children there, see the same people, and form friendships. It generates an interest in books in the children. They get to sit, read, relax and calm their nervous system away from technology and screen time, encouraging creativity and imagination. For the next age group, kids who may not have a computer or tablet can go to a library after school somewhere peaceful to study, find answers and build independence. It is a hub for people in general to meet, learn, spread ideas and encourage creativity.
