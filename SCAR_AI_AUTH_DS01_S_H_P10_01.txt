Online learning offers benefits as well as drawbacks. Firstly you get full flexibility, you choose when and how you will learn and you set the pace too. This naturally leads on to choosing where you learn, meaning it is truly cost effective with no need to travel to a place of study which is also time efficient. The learning materials are accessible at all times, this allows students to learn from anywhere, so you can set up your environment to your specification and make it as comfortable as you like. The downsides include less human interaction, the requirement for strong self discipline with no teachers or tutors to push you to catch up if you fall behind, and you need to self monitor and self test to be able to realise early on if you have missed anything critical. There is potential for isolation as there is a distinct lack of human interaction with the genuine possibility of depression. A student is reliant on their own technology which is a cost and a consideration in itself. Learning from home is best suited to self motivated people who can balance learning and commitments.
