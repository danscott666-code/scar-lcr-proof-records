Preparing for a job interview is fundamentally about reducing uncertainty and demonstrating controlled competence under scrutiny. Start by developing a precise understanding of the role, the company's commercial position, and how your experience maps directly onto their needs. Generic knowledge is useless; you need targeted insight into their products, competitors, and current challenges. From there, refine your narrative so that every example you give has a clear structure, showing not just what you did, but why it mattered and what measurable impact it had. You should also anticipate pressure points. Identify gaps in your experience or CV and decide in advance how you will address them without sounding defensive. Rehearsal is critical, but it must be deliberate rather than robotic; practice articulating answers clearly while maintaining a natural tone. Pay attention to how you present yourself physically, as first impressions are formed quickly and are difficult to reverse. Finally, prepare intelligent questions that signal genuine interest and commercial awareness. An interview is not just an evaluation of you, but also your opportunity to assess whether the environment is worth committing to. The strongest candidates come across as prepared, composed, and purposeful rather than overly polished or rehearsed.
