Like anything in life, there are always pros and cons, and this is also true of online learning. With online learning there is a lot of freedom and flexibility to choose when you learn and you can set your own pace so you do not become overwhelmed. Conversely, you can end up not keeping up with the required standard and not realising you are falling behind because there is no one to bounce ideas off. So there is a balance to be struck. There is also the fact that you do not need to travel anywhere. The learning material is available online twenty four hours a day, and you can pick and choose when you study, fitting learning around your other commitments including family. The downsides are that there is no face to face learning with a teacher, and you may feel isolated at times. There is no one gauging your progress and giving you motivation to drive you forward, especially when you do not feel like studying. You need to be self driven and self motivated, and make sure that other aspects of your life do not consistently overtake the time you would need for learning. There is real evidence to show that the lack of face to face interaction can lead to depression and loneliness.
