Preparing for a job interview is not optional in my opinion, it is absolutely compulsory and critical. There is no serious employer that would want to take on a candidate who was not fully prepared. That means the person needs a high quality, up to date CV that includes relevant qualifications and work experience for the role they are applying for. Including interests and hobbies also shows that you are a person of substance and character. It is important that the candidate does research into the company to understand how it works, what it would want from them, the company ethos and ethics, and what kind of role they would be fulfilling. When speaking to the interviewer, this research shows that the candidate has a good knowledge base, can speak at a high level about the role, and understands how they will be expected to fulfil it. It confirms to the interviewer that the candidate has a level of care and preparation, which are admirable qualities. They should also try to understand the interview process and any criteria required. Practising a mock interview can be helpful, particularly if tests are included. At minimum the candidate should have a few questions of their own ready regarding the role and company.
