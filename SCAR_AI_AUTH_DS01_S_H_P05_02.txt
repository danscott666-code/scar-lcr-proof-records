I think a library can help a local community as a hub and as a knowledge base, as somewhere that is safe, friendly, calming and away from day to day life and stress. Somewhere for kids away from screen time whether computers, tablets or televisions. Somewhere you can genuinely relax and learn, develop life skills, find books using a referencing system, and somewhere that is available and open to everyone who respects the environment. Particularly good I believe for parents with young children who can attend events held there for similar minded parents. This can start to structure how a child thinks about learning and reading and how to access information that is not just instant internet searches. Then you have older kids doing GCSEs and A levels that need to get away from a noisy or distracting home and go there as a sanctuary. This progresses on to college and university students in the same way, somewhere to focus without distraction. All the way through to adulthood and then the elderly who may enjoy coffee mornings, book reading and discussion clubs. There is something for everyone that is local, dependable, safe and open to all.
