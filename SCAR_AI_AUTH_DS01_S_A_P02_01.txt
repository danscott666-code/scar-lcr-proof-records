One effective time management method for a student preparing for exams is the time blocking approach combined with focused study intervals. The student begins by mapping out all subjects and topics that need to be covered, then assigns specific blocks of time in a daily schedule to each area based on priority and difficulty. Instead of vague intentions like revise maths, each block is tied to a clear objective, such as completing a set of problems or reviewing a chapter. Within each block, the student works in short, intense bursts, typically 25 to 50 minutes, followed by a brief break to maintain concentration and avoid burnout. This structure reduces procrastination because the workload feels contained and manageable. It also creates a sense of accountability, as each time slot has a defined purpose. Over time, the student reviews progress and adjusts the schedule, allocating more time to weaker areas while maintaining regular revision of stronger ones. Consistency is the key advantage of this method, as it turns revision into a routine rather than a last-minute effort, leading to better retention, reduced stress, and a more controlled, systematic preparation process.
