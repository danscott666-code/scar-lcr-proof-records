A practical and effective time management method for exam preparation is time blocking, where a student allocates specific periods of the day to focused study sessions for particular subjects. Instead of vaguely planning to revise maths at some point, the student commits to a defined slot, such as 4:00 to 5:30 p.m., dedicated solely to algebra practice. This approach forces clarity and reduces procrastination because each block has a clear objective. To make it work, the student should first map out all subjects and topics, then distribute them across the week in realistic chunks, balancing difficult and easier material to avoid burnout. Each study block should be protected from distractions, with phones silenced and unnecessary tabs closed, creating a controlled environment that supports deep concentration. Breaks are deliberately scheduled between blocks, allowing the brain to reset and maintain high performance over longer periods. At the end of each day, the student reviews what was completed and adjusts the next day's plan if necessary, ensuring continuous improvement rather than rigid adherence to a failing schedule. Over time, this method builds discipline, reduces last-minute cramming, and provides a structured path towards mastering the full syllabus before exams.
