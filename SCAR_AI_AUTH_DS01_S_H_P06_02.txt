Road congestion in cities generally stems from several main factors, too many cars and not enough road space. This is particularly true and multiplies exponentially during rush hours and peak times. Couple this with badly designed infrastructure that leads drivers into bottlenecks, increasing pressure on already busy roads. Bad weather considerably slows drivers down because in my opinion many drivers are not confident due to their lack of ability and are simply poor drivers. Another result of people being poor drivers is accidents, which when there is no management to speak of really slows traffic down, and not only because of a blocked lane but also because other drivers feel compelled to rubber neck selfishly and slow everyone else's progress. People not trusting public transport, not wanting to pay high fares, or simply preferring the comfort of their own vehicle results in gridlock and traffic mayhem. Road etiquette is poor and drivers have little to no common sense the majority of the time. People simply cannot navigate situations that arise in the moment outside their usual daily commute, and when one person shuts down at the head of the queue it has a huge knock on effect on everyone behind them.
