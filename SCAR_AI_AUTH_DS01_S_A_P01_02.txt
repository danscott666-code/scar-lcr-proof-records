Making a cup of tea starts with fresh cold water, as water that has been previously boiled tends to produce a flat taste. Fill a kettle and bring it to a full boil. While the water is heating, take a clean mug and, if you want a better result, warm it by swirling a little hot water inside and then discarding it. Place a teabag, such as English Breakfast or Assam, into the empty mug. Once the water has boiled, pour it directly over the teabag, ensuring it is fully submerged so the flavour extracts properly. Let the tea steep for a few minutes, depending on how strong you prefer it; a longer steep produces a richer, more robust taste. Gently stir the teabag, then remove it, pressing it lightly against the side of the mug if you want to extract more flavour. At this point, add milk if desired, pouring a small amount and adjusting to your preference. If you like your tea sweet, stir in sugar or another sweetener. Finally, give it a brief stir, allow it to cool slightly, and drink while it is still hot.
