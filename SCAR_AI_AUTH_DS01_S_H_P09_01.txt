One reason that recycling systems fail is due to them being designed to run for profit rather than to be environmentally conscientious, which seems contrary to the whole idea of recycling in the first instance, that is protecting the planet and its resources. Often it is cheaper and more cost efficient to manufacture new products rather than recycle old ones, and this comes down to outdated infrastructure and processes, confusion as to what is actually recyclable by the end user, high contamination rates from food residue for example, and the inability to separate multi material packaging. This means that more often than not whole loads of waste which should and could be recycled are sent to landfill or incineration and end up as a total dead loss. Even when items are successfully recycled there is sometimes no demand for the end product and it has to be stockpiled and eventually sent to landfill. The issue of recycling failing is often made worse by the many varying collection systems across the UK. I have lived in three different areas within a ten minute drive of each other and each area has different colour bins requiring different types of waste, which can easily lead to confusion for many people trying to recycle correctly.
