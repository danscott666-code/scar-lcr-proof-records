There can be many benefits to working from home but also almost an equal number of negatives. No commute means you can save time and money on transportation but alternatively you are almost trapped in your house with no external interaction which can lead to mental health issues over the longer term. Having flexibility with time allows you to form your work day around personal commitments like childcare and appointments but the downside is that you may become distracted by a partner just being there or having the expectation you can do more around the house because you are home, or if you have children they may not respect your work boundaries. If being fair, work colleagues could often be a distraction too but on the upside you get to interact and socialise and often colleagues are friends so it can brighten your day. Being at home means you can control your work environment including temperature, lighting and furniture for added comfort. The downside is that home furniture is not designed for prolonged periods of sitting and working and can lead to discomfort or injury like back ache. Working at home also means you are liable for the cost of running any devices you require resulting in increased usage of electricity, heating and internet.
