I believe walking can improve general health, but with the caveat that you would need to do a sufficient amount of steps in any one day for it to start to be effective. I would also say that this cannot be on a one off basis and would need to be maintained over a long term duration. You would also need to get your heart rate up to a certain degree, to start getting your heart pumping and actually act as a cardio workout, and this I do not believe would be possible over very short distances. So for me, I generally try to get 12,500 steps in a day, which is over the daily recommended amount of 10,000. With that amount of steps, it actually helps me to gain points which increases my level with my life insurance, and a discount is applied to my life insurance policy premium but only if I maintain this number of steps every day over the whole year. When you are doing cardiovascular exercise this gets your heart working, which can reduce heart disease and many other forms of disease and illness. It keeps your muscles strong and keeps you from becoming weak and vulnerable to other illnesses and diseases at the same time.
