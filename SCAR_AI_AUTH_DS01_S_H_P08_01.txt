The first step of preparing for an interview is to write or update your CV. This should include all your academic achievements and all your previous work experience with durations and dates, and it should also include references that any potential employer can contact to find out more about you as a candidate for the role. A good way to make sure you get your CV up to date and in a correct and appealing format is to use an online CV builder and checker. Something particularly important is to highlight and include all previous relevant experience you have had for the job you are interviewing for. A good way to make sure you include relevant information is to research the new company and attempt to find out the interview process, who the interviewer will be and what type of interview they conduct, whether there are any tests, and to understand the ethos of the company and find out where and how you align with their ethics. It is also a good idea to have your own set of questions for the interviewer, have a five year plan ready to show drive and ambition, and have a reason as to why you would like to work for their company.
